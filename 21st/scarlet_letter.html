DIMMESDALE is in the midst of his sermon
Dimmesdale
And the people of New England shall be chosen by God himself!
The crowd cheers.
Crowd character a
(Proud)
Never on this land has stood the man so honored by his mortal brethren, as the preacher!
DIMMESDALE struggles to keep himself standing, as if about to fall on the ground.
Two men from the crowd tries to help him.
DIMMESDALE pushes the men aside with force.
The crowd murmurs.
DIMMESDALE sees HESTER in the crowd.
DIMMESDALE
come hither! Come, my little Pearl!
HESTER starts to walk towards DIMMESDALE, holding PEARL's hand.
CHILLINGWORTH grabs HESTER's arm.
ChillingwoRTH
Madman, hold! what is your purpose?
Hester pulls away her arm from CHILLINGWORTH's grip.
CHILLINGWORTH
Wave back that woman! Cast of this child! All shall be well! I can yet save you! Why would you bring infamy on your sacred profession?
DIMMESDALE
Hester Prynne...
DIMMESDALE lets out a small cry of pain.
DimmESDALE
In the name of Him who gives me grace at this last moment, come hither now! Come Hester! Support me up yonder scaffold!
CHILLINGWORTH shouts.
2.
Chillingworth
Thou hide from the crooks of the earth, there is no one place so secret where thou could have escaped me, save in this very scaffold!
Dimmesdale
Temper! Methinks thou art too late!
DIMMESDALE shifts his focus away from CHILLINGWORTH.
DimmESDALE
(solemn)
People of New England! Behold me here, the one sinner of the world! At last! I finally stand where I should have been seven years since.
The crowd starts to murmur once again.
DIMMESDALE
I am the father of Pearl!
DIMMESDALE shows the scarlet letter on his chest.
The crowd lets out an audible gasp.
DIMMESDALE struggles to keep on standing. He falls to the ground.
DIMMESDALE
Thou has escaped me! Thou has escaped me! May God forgive thee, for I have deeply sinned!
DIMMESDALE lets out a sound of agony, pain.
DIMMESDALE
My little Pearl...
DIMMESDALE brushes his hands on PEARL's cheeks.
Pearl leans in to hug his dying father.
DIMMESDALE
Hester, dear, farewell...
Hester
(sad)
Shall we not meet again? Shall we not spend our afterlife together? Surely we have ransomed one another with all this woe!
HESTER tears up.
3.
DIMMESDALE
Quiet, Hester. We broke the law, we have sinned, and we cannot be together.
DIMMESDALE closes his eyes finally.
HESTER stares in awe.
